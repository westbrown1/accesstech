Health Benefits of Magnesium.

Positive Health Effects of Magnesium, The Super Mineral.



An estimated 80% of the human population is deficient in the essential mineral magnesium. Magnesium is involved in over 300 metabolic reactions. Magnesium is crucial in ATP (adenosine triphosphate) synthesis within the mitochondria of a cell . Magnesium benefits are associated with stroke and heart health, high blood pressure, asthma, bone and teeth health (osteoporosis), collagen production (healthy skin, hair), type 2 diabetes (improved insulin sensitivity), weight control (improved insulin sensitivity), fatigue (maintaining energy throughout the day), anxiety, muscle cramping, blood coagulation, headaches, sleep (muscle relaxation), constipation. Yeah, it's a super mineral. Many health experts say it is the most important dietary mineral. However, too much of a good thing is not so good. It's a laxative so diarrhea is a sign of too much. I take any magnesium supplements later at night when I know I'm not going out. RDA for men is +-410 mg and +-360 mg for women. The tolerable upper intake supplementation is 350 mg/day for adults. The Linus Pauling Institute is a good source of infomation on magnesium. It is the source for most of this information.

Good food sources of magnesium include spinach, cereal, brown rice, almonds, Swiss chard (cooked), peas, avocados, oatmeal, broccoli, bananas, kidney beans, dark chocolate.
